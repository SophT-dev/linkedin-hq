## THE ROUTINE (fits on a sticky note)

### Daily — 15 min
1. Open `/news` on your phone. Star 2-3 things that made you react. Skip everything else.
2. Comment on 3-5 posts from the engagement list (Nick Abraham, Enzo, Eric N., Michel Lieben, LGJ — whoever posted today).
3. Drop into r/coldemail briefly. Reply to 1-2 threads if something's obviously relevant.
4. Reply to any comments on Taha's own posts.
5. Send 5-10 ICP connection requests (B2B founders, agency owners, SaaS operators).

### Monday — 20 min
- Add any new wins from last week to WinsLog. 2 sentences per win. That's it.
- Glance at what you starred. If nothing stands out for a post, that's fine.

### First Monday of the month — 2 hours
- Run `/linkedin-batch` → generate 5 posts
- Pick 2 for lead magnets
- Queue all 5 with dates in the Posts tab
- Done for the month

**Nothing else is required. Everything else on this page is reference material.**

## CONTENT FORMAT CHEAT SHEET

Extracted from the 43 post types. Use this when choosing format in Phase 4.

| Format | When to use | Engagement signal |
|---|---|---|
| Stat-first listicle | When you have a real number to lead with | High saves |
| "Old Way vs New Way" | When a mainstream practice has a better alternative | High comments/debate |
| Personal story (chronological) | When there's a real client win or loss | Highest engagement (4x avg) |
| Contrarian take + 3-4 points | When taking a strong stance on a debated topic | Comments + shares |
| "Which would you choose?" (A vs B) | When you have two actual cold email examples to compare | Easy to comment on |
| ICP empathy post | When you want to make a specific audience feel seen | Saves + DMs |
| Negative bias listicle | "Top 10 worst..." or "Stop doing..." | Outperforms positive framing consistently |
| Rant / conversational | When there's genuine frustration worth sharing | Comments/discourse |
| Blueprint/playbook | When there's a full process worth documenting | Saves + reposts |
| Curiosity + payoff at end | Open loop hook, don't resolve until last paragraph | Time-on-post, shares |

**Principle from MrBeast applied to LinkedIn:** the hook's job is to make *not reading* feel like a loss. Open the loop, raise the stakes, pay it off at the end — never in the second paragraph.

**Negative bias > positive framing:** "Top 10 worst mistakes" consistently outperforms "Top 10 best practices." Use this when choosing between a positive and negative framing of the same insight.
