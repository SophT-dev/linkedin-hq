## THE 7 LAWS (what he actually proved)

### 1. Study great posts before writing a single line
You can't beat a standard you've never seen. Most people post for months and never improve because they have **no baseline to strive for.**
- **His method:** Taplio (search viral posts by *topic*) or Scribe (search viral posts by *creator*). Filter: min 300 likes, last 30 days. Study the hook, format, length, tone.
- **Creator-search > topic-search** — topic is too wide; picking specific creators guarantees a quality baseline.

### 2. Visuals beat hooks — spend MORE time on the visual
This is the one most people get backwards. Posts with great visuals get **2-10x the engagement.**
- On mobile, the hook is 2 lines of tiny real estate. A vertical image (**1080 × 1350**) fills the entire screen and stops the scroll.
- The image is a **second hook** — put a title/subhook *inside* the image.
- A strong infographic can be the post on its own (his friend Pierre Ariel does one hook line + huge infographic, performs great).
- **Every single one of his and his team's posts has a visual.** No exceptions.

### 3. Repurpose your biggest hits — relentlessly
Most people forgot your old post. Your new followers never saw it.
- His tool-stack infographic has been reposted 5-6 times, hits 100K+ impressions *every time.*
- **Don't just repost — rewrite slightly better.** His upgrade: replaced vague claims with precise numbers ("we generate 100+ leads/month via LinkedIn," "35,000+ site visitors/month"). Same structure, 150K → ~200K impressions.
- **The compounding calendar:** by month 5 you reuse 4 winners from month 1 + 4 from month 3, so you only write ~12 truly new posts to hit 20/month. Consistency gets *easier* over time.

### 4. Consistency is the whole game — it's trust-building before lead-gen
- Most people quit before results. You won't know what a great post looks like until ~100 posts.
- His story: committed to 5x/week for 3 months. **30 posts, zero leads.** Post 31 (national holiday, headache, 10pm, almost skipped it) → went viral: 600+ likes, 140K impressions, **25 meetings, 4 new clients, 2→6 clients from one post.**
- Reframe: LinkedIn benefits *all* your other channels. Cold prospect gets your email → checks your profile → sees 50 posts of expertise → converts. **It closes existing leads even when it generates none directly.**

### 5. Plan ahead with recurring formats
- A content calendar with **repeatable formats** you publish week after week (he tags them with emojis). Makes consistency effortless.
- His proven recurring formats:
  - **Internal milestone breakdown** — "we booked X meetings last month, here's how" / revenue closed + sales process / conversion rate increase + breakdown.
  - **Tool category roundup** — "AI agents I use," "data scraping tools," "all-in-one prospecting tools."

### 6. Go DEEP — don't fear the character limit
- Long, in-depth posts get *fewer* likes than memes/selfies but **sign far more clients.**
- His best posts for BOTH reach and conversion hit the character limit.
- **10K followers can out-earn 100K followers.** Many 60-100K creators make <$10K/mo; done right, 10K followers → $100K+/mo. (He cites Adam Robinson, Guillaume Moubeche as the depth standard.)

### 7. Distribution isn't just the algorithm — two manual levers
- **Connect with your ICP directly.** Send connection requests to everyone matching your ICP (LinkedIn shows company size, title, industry, geo). Once connected, your posts get shown to them. Hundreds of ideal clients regularly reading your insights → conversions.
- **Comments are a growth channel, not an afterthought.** An insightful comment under a big creator's post often gets seen by **more people than your own post** (LinkedIn now shows impressions on comments). Experiment result: posting + commenting > either alone, and **commenting-only beat posting-only** for profile views + followers.
  - Tool: **engagement lists** — build a custom LinkedIn feed of only chosen creators (search bar → Posts → pick people → save the URL) so you comment efficiently instead of wading through garbage.
  - **Reply to every comment on your own post in the first 30 min** — the algorithm uses early engagement to decide between showing your post to 1,000 vs 10,000 people.

## ANATOMY OF A GREAT POST (his 5 ingredients, in priority order)

1. **Proven angle / great idea** — the MrBeast principle: two people spend equal time, one gets 10K views, one gets 1M, the difference is the *idea.* Ideation is the highest-leverage activity. Spend disproportionate time here.
2. **Depth + real insight** — give away the whole thing. His tool-stack post gave the *entire* stack, not a teaser.
3. **Attention-grabbing hook (first 2 lines)** — the only thing visible before "see more."
   - **The big-number formula:** lead with a credibility number that answers "why should I care?"
   - His example: *"Lemlist recently crossed $34M in ARR. Here's every tool they use to run their SaaS."* The number makes you care about a company you may not know.
4. **Captivating visual (1080 × 1350)** — fills the screen, second hook inside it, infographic can stand alone.
5. **Readable structure** — spacing, line breaks, light formatting. Never a wall of text.

## EMPLOYEE-LED MOTION (video 2) — applied to a 3-person team

He scaled by turning 24 employees into creators. We're 3 people, but the mechanics still apply to **Taha + Sophiya as a dual-brand**, with Shahwaiz as a possible third later.

- **Incentivize posting** — quarterly competition (most posts above 20 likes). A $1K prize generated $80K MRR for him. *(For us: not a cash competition yet, but the principle — make posting a tracked, rewarded habit, not a someday task.)*
- **Make it effortless** — team reuses the founder's winning posts + proven angles; a **designer** handles visuals so creators only write. *(For us: I supply angle + visual brief so you only approve + lightly edit. This is exactly our approval-first flow.)*
- **Train to a standard** — show what "great" looks like. One unlock took Ian from 40 likes to 150+ consistently, 4K → 19K followers.
- **Starts at the top** — founder leads by example. Taha's profile must be the most active.
- **Hire people who already want to grow** — future hires who post become compounding distribution.
- **It compounds** — each person who succeeds builds conviction for the next.
