# Sub-skill: post structure selection

Read by `linkedin-post/SKILL.md` Phase 3, right after `hook-selection.md`. Job: pick a real,
proven **layout** for the post body — separate from the hook, separate from the content angle
(F1-F12). This is the dimension that was missing before 2026-07-08: a post can have a great hook
and still read as generic if the paragraph rhythm doesn't match anything real.

**Standing rule (Sophiya's call, 2026-07-08): no original structures either, same as hooks.**
Every draft picks one named structure from `playbook/POST-STRUCTURE-LIBRARY.md` and follows its
rhythm rules exactly — sentence length, spacing, bullet/arrow style, number density. Don't blend
two structures in one post, and don't invent a new one yet.

## The five proven structures (full detail in POST-STRUCTURE-LIBRARY.md)

| Structure | Read | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **S1** Numbered Tactical List | Dense but broken into chunks, `-` sub-bullets, `↳` takeaway arrows, numbers everywhere | F4, F12 — synthesis/playbook content |
| **S2** One-Line Cadence | Near one-thought-per-line, repetition for rhythm, almost no bullets/numbers | F6 — emotional/personal stories |
| **S3** Emoji-Numbered System Breakdown | 1️⃣2️⃣3️⃣ sections, `→` arrow sub-points, ends in a "Tools used:" list | F2 — tool-stack/system posts |
| **S4** Percentage Countdown | Every point is an "X > Y" comparison with a specific % lift + arrow takeaway | Contrarian/benchmark content |
| **S5** Confrontation Scene | Opens mid-action, dialogue-driven, ends on a 1-2 line reframe | F5, F6 — one dramatic moment |

## How to pick

1. Match the **content format** (F1-F12, from `hook-selection.md`'s output) to the table above —
   it's a strong default, not a hard rule. F12 Synthesis almost always wants S1. F6 Emotional
   Story almost always wants S2 or S5.
2. Check the **assigned angle** actually has the raw material a structure needs. S1 needs 4+ real
   sub-points with specifics. S4 needs real percentages to cite — don't force a comparison
   structure onto content with no real lift numbers behind it (that's inventing data, banned).
3. If genuinely torn between two, pick the one with more real numbers to plug in — per the
   standing note that people respond to specific numbers, lean into density where the real data
   supports it.

## What to hand back to the orchestrator

Which structure (S1-S5) was used, and confirm the body actually follows its rhythm rules — not
just "inspired by." If a draft mixes structures (e.g. starts as S1 but drifts into flowing S2
prose halfway through), that's a fail — rewrite to commit to one.

## Guardrail

If the assigned angle doesn't cleanly fit any of the five yet, say so and flag it — don't force a
mismatch. This library grows over time as more structures get identified from the corpus.
