## Executive summary

Taha's LinkedIn is a **near-complete growth machine that is switched off.** After ~2 years and 9 years in AI, the account sits at **6,795 followers**, **~0.2% engagement**, **939 impressions/week**, **3 profile views/day**, and a **~1% winner rate** — with the single highest-leverage lever, **commenting, completely dormant (0 comments in 14 days).**

This is not a talent problem or a content problem. It is a **consistency + distribution** problem, and every fix is 100% in our control. We already own more infrastructure than 99% of creators (a corpus of 6,642 analysed posts, two AI drafting skills, a live comment-suggestion tool, a free connection-targeting system, a zero-cost visual pipeline, ~50 competitor lead-magnets, and a full analytics dashboard). We are using almost none of it.

**The proposal:** a 14-day Phase-1 sprint that turns the engine on — **4 posts/week, ~130 comments, ~130 targeted connections** — using the assets we already built, backed at every step by what the top LinkedIn operators (Lara Acosta, Pierre Herubel, Adam Robinson, Justin Welsh, Jasmin Alić, Ruben Hassid, Casey Brown, Michel Lieben) and LinkedIn's own 2025 algorithm research prove works. We do **not** chase followers in these two weeks — we convert impressions and profile views into **DMs and pipeline.** Video is Phase 2; we earn it by lighting the fire first.

> **The one-line bet:** Off → 4 posts/week + ~130 comments + ~130 connections over 14 days. Prove the engine repeats, then pour fuel on a fire that's already lit.

**Validated against every competitor.** We reverse-engineered the top-10 *and* worst-10 posts of all 12 top cold-email/outbound creators we track (see the companion *Competitor Strategy Teardown*). Three findings underwrite this plan: (1) the **comment-to-DM lead magnet** ("comment KEYWORD + connect → DM the asset") is the single universal growth mechanic — it drove **500–3,196 comments** on the winners; (2) every winner **owns one contrarian wedge** and repeats it (ours: *signal over volume, systems over copy*); and (3) the losers' worst posts are *exactly Taha's current habit* — dry tips or announcements with **no hook, no proof-number, no comment-gate**, off a consistent lane. This plan does the opposite of the flops and the same as the winners.

## 5. The commenting system — the #1 lever (currently OFF)

> This is the deepest-leverage tactic and the one nobody can throttle. Jasmin Alić: *"Post 7×/week max, but comment 20–30×/day."* Casey Brown built a 40K audience where **90% of clients come inbound** off this — one comment thread became **$1.2M.** We leave **0** comments. Turning this on is the single biggest fix in this document.

**The mindset — peer, not fan (Casey Brown):** comment on **5–15 posts/day, not 50.** Before each one, run the *coffee-shop test*: "if I were sitting across from this person, what would I actually say?" **Add insight or a strategic question — never praise.** Praise makes you a fan; insight makes you a peer, and clients hire peers. **Never sell in comments.**

**The Power List (Casey Brown):** one Google Sheet, 4 columns (category · name · profile · last-engaged), **~30 ICP founders + 15 adjacent (RevOps/sales-ops) + 10 cold-email peers + 5 macro (e.g. Adam Robinson).** We already have `capture-connects.mjs` that mines commenters into a Connects tab — same principle, automated.

### Exactly what to say — the 4 comment types, with real Taha examples

Every comment is **15+ words** (15+-word comments ≈ 2× impact) and posted in the **first 30–60 minutes** (be one of the first 3–5 comments — early engagement is weighted heaviest).

**1 · Value-add** — expand the post with our own data/tactic:
> *"We saw the same thing — cutting a client from 4k to 900 emails/day and triggering on live job-change signals took positive replies from 8% to 32%. Fewer sends, better inbox placement, more meetings. Volume was hiding the real problem."*

**2 · Challenge** — respectfully push back, make them reconsider:
> *"Genuine pushback: doesn't more personalization just move the bottleneck? We A/B'd {first_name} + 'saw your post' against pure relevance-triggered copy — the theatrical stuff didn't move positive-reply rate at all. What are you seeing?"*

**3 · Supportive** — validate, then layer a deeper insight from our build:
> *"100% on deliverability being the whole game. The part people miss: we run domain warmup + SEG monitoring as an automated Clay→n8n loop, so a burned domain gets pulled before it tanks a campaign. Boring infra, everything."*

**4 · Witty/relatable** — when it fits; people remember how you made them feel:
> *"'Just send more emails' is the 'have you tried turning it off and on again' of outbound. We all did it, we all burned a domain, we all learned."*

### The rituals + the data
- **Michel Lieben's 30/30:** 30 min engaging *before* posting; reply to *every* comment on your own post in the *first 30 min* after.
- **The 15-min daily block:** 10 min comment on 5–7 Power-List posts · 5 min reply to your own comments · +5 min move promising threads to DMs · Monday 10 min update the Power List. **Commit 90 days.**
- **Data:** replying to your own comments ≈ **+30% engagement**; 10 comments/day for a month ≈ **+40% profile visits**; reply within minutes during the golden hour ≈ *doubles* comment count when the algorithm is judging.
- **Comment → lead pipeline:** capture commenters (Trigify) → enrich (Clay) → filter to ICP → warm DM (HeyReach). They already engaged, so acceptance and reply crush cold outbound. *(Needs Sophiya's sign-off — same gate as anything that sends.)*

## 6. Connecting

**The play (Michel Lieben, 100k-conversation dataset):** send **10–20 personalized ICP requests/day**, but warm the target first — a **profile view / like / follow before the request lifts acceptance by +30.2%.** Never pitch in the request (Lara Acosta: reference their content + an overlap + a light nod).

**Script:**
> *"Hey [Name] — your post on [specific point, e.g. killing volume to fix deliverability] hit home. We run cold-email systems for B2B founders and I've been making the same argument with our own campaign numbers. Would be good to have you in my network."*

## 7. DMs — attraction, not pitch-slapping (per scenario)

> The frame (Jasmin Alić + Lara Acosta): *"the selling comes from the sharing."* Lara's **"ask permission to sell" / EduSales** — soft conversation *after* they engage. Hard rule: **60-day patience — no pitch for 60 days after connecting.** ~1 in 10 touches sells; the other 9 build credibility (Adam Robinson).

- **(a) Comment-thread → DM:** *"Hey [Name] — liked your angle on [thing]. Genuinely curious, not pitching: how's outbound running at [Company] right now — in-house, or stalled?"*
- **(b) Lead-magnet delivery:** *"Here's the Cold Email System Audit you asked for → [link]. It scores your setup on the 5 things that predict reply rate. Once you run it — what part of your outbound are you least happy with?"*
- **(c) Re-engage a profile viewer:** *"Saw you stopped by my profile [Name] — no agenda. If something in your outbound isn't landing, happy to be a sounding board. What are you working on?"*
- **(d) Warm follow-up (proof):** *"You've been following the cold-email systems stuff, so it's clearly on your mind. We just pulled $42K pipeline in 10 days off 896 emails for a client doing exactly [their situation]. Happy to walk you through how the system's built — no pitch, just the mechanics. Worth 15 min?"*

> **The rule that makes it work:** posts are drafted the *night before* (or batch-drafted Sunday). The morning session is for *distribution*, never for staring at a blank draft.
