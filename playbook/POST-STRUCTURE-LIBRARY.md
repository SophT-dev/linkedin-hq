# Post Structure Library — the layout swipe file

**What this is, and why it's separate from FORMAT-LIBRARY.md:** FORMAT-LIBRARY.md catalogs
*content angles* (F1 Big Number, F8 Contrarian, etc.) — what the post is about and why it pulls.
This doc catalogs *physical layout* — how the words sit on the screen: line length, white space,
bullets vs. arrows, brick vs. broken, number density. A post can be F1 (Big Number) content
wrapped in S1 (Numbered Tactical List) structure, or the same F1 content wrapped in S2 (One-Line
Cadence) structure — same angle, completely different read. Drafting needs both dimensions
picked deliberately, not just the content angle.

**Standing rule (Sophiya's call, 2026-07-08): for the first few weeks, every draft uses a
template from BOTH this doc and the hook bank — never a freehand structure.** The goal is
consistency with what's proven to work before we earn the right to improvise. See
`hook-selection.md` and `post-structure-selection.md` (the sub-skills that enforce this).

Built by reading ~60 real high-signal posts (100+ likes, tagged high-signal) across Michel
Lieben, Josh Braun, Kenny Damian — the same corpus behind the Template Library.

## S1 · The Numbered Tactical List (Michel Lieben's default)

**Skeleton:**
```
[Big claim or number, one line.]

([Parenthetical credibility stat — how many X analyzed/processed/hit])

[One-line transition, often ending in a directional arrow] ↓

1. [Bold-weight sub-header, one line]

[2-4 lines of explanation, plain prose, blank lines between logical beats — never a solid block]

- [sub-bullet: specific tool/tactic]
- [sub-bullet: specific tool/tactic]

↳ [the one-line actionable takeaway or "what changes next period"]


2. [next numbered section, same shape]

...

[Direct question to the reader] 👇

P.S: [secondary hook or soft CTA, often with a link]
```

**Rhythm rules:**
- Numbers everywhere — dollar figures, percentages, counts. Nearly every section has at least one
  specific number, never a vague claim.
- Blank line between every beat. Never more than 4-5 lines of prose before a break.
- Sub-bullets use a plain dash `-`, not bullets or emoji.
- `↳` is a specific, recurring glyph for "here's the takeaway from what I just said" — don't
  substitute an arbitrary arrow, this exact one recurs across dozens of posts.
- Sections are numbered `1.` `2.` `3.` (plain) or `1️⃣` `2️⃣` `3️⃣` (emoji) — pick one style per post,
  never mix within a single post.

**Proven —** Michel Lieben, "How it started: 4,000 cold emails sent. 1 lead generated... How
it's going: $4M+ ARR" (2794 likes/801 comments) · "This GTM workflow booked us 138 meetings in
July" (626 likes/203 comments) · "How I would find my first 10 clients if I had to restart from
0" (876 likes/206 comments, 473 likes/184 comments — reused this exact structure at least twice).

## S2 · The One-Line Cadence (Josh Braun's default — the opposite of S1)

**Skeleton:**
```
[Scene-setting line or a direct claim, standalone.]

[One-clause line.]
[One-clause line.]

[A quoted line of dialogue, its own paragraph.]

[Narration, 3-6 words.]
[Narration, 3-6 words.]
[Narration, 3-6 words.]
[Narration, 3-6 words.]   <- repetition of a word/short phrase across consecutive lines, for rhythm

[The reframe — one line that flips how the reader sees everything above it.]

[The lesson, 1-2 lines, often a small metaphor.]
```

**Rhythm rules:**
- Almost every sentence, sometimes every clause, is its own line. This is a wall of *white
  space*, the opposite of brick.
- Average line length: 3-8 words. If a line has more than ~12 words, break it.
- Repetition-for-rhythm is a deliberate device: the same word or near-identical short phrase
  repeated on 3-4 consecutive lines ("Withdrawal. Withdrawal. Withdrawal. Withdrawal." / "No
  pitch. No jargon. Just presence.").
- Dialogue is formatted as alternating short lines in quotes, no "he said/she said" — just the
  quote, its own line.
- Almost no bullets, arrows, or numbers. The structure IS the content here — don't force a list
  into this shape.
- Ends on a 1-2 line reframe or metaphor, not a CTA. The lesson is the hook's payoff.

**Proven —** Josh Braun, "My friend's a CEO. He hangs up on 99% of cold calls." (1457
likes/136 comments) · "'I'm just following up.'... Withdrawal. Withdrawal. Withdrawal.
Withdrawal." (905 likes/77 comments) · "20 seconds into the cold call, the prospect says, 'I'm
not interested.'" (345 likes/44 comments).

## S3 · The Emoji-Numbered System Breakdown

**Skeleton:**
```
[Big number/dollar claim, one line.]

[One-line transition] 👇

1️⃣ [ALL-CAPS or bold mini-header]

[1-2 lines context]

→ [sub-point]
→ [sub-point]
→ [sub-point]

Tools used:
→ [tool]: [what it's for]
→ [tool]: [what it's for]


2️⃣ [next section, same shape]

...

[Direct question] 👇

P.S: [secondary hook/link]
```

**Rhythm rules:**
- Number emoji (1️⃣2️⃣3️⃣), not plain numbers — visually distinct from S1's plain-number variant,
  used interchangeably by the same author depending on post, but never mixed within one post.
- Sub-points use `→` arrows, not dashes — this is the tell that distinguishes S3 from S1's `-`
  dash sub-bullets.
- Almost always ends with an explicit "Tools used:" or "Software used:" sub-list — this is a
  recurring, deliberate device for the tool-stack-style post (pairs naturally with F2 Tool Stack).

**Proven —** Kenny Damian, "I've spent 600+ hours inside Claude Code" (1033 likes/88 comments) ·
Michel Lieben, "What our $6,500,000/year Funnel looks like" (573 likes/185 comments) · "We added
$715,200 ARR in June" (446 likes/165 comments).

## S4 · The Percentage Countdown

**Skeleton:**
```
[Claim: "How to do X like the top 1%"]

([Parenthetical: "After analyzing N conversations/emails/DMs in <tool>"])

[Transition line]

1. [X] > [Y]   <- a direct comparison as the sub-header, not a description

[One line stating the specific percentage lift/stat]

→ [one-line actionable takeaway]


2. [next comparison]
...

[Direct question] 👇

P.S: [secondary hook]
```

**Rhythm rules:**
- Every single point is a comparison ("Short > Long", "Personalization > Volume") — this
  X > Y framing is the structural signature, not optional.
- A specific percentage in nearly every point (22% more replies, 42% better, +30.2% acceptance).
  If you can't cite a real percentage for a point, this isn't the right structure for that point.
- The `→` takeaway line is mandatory per point — never leave a stat without the "so do this"
  follow-through.

**Proven —** Michel Lieben, "How the top 1% make LinkedIn Outreach work in 2025" (372
likes/146 comments) · "How to do LinkedIn Outreach like the top 1%" (420 likes/131 comments) ·
"How to run email outreach like the top 1%" (385 likes/193 comments) — same author, same
structure, reused at least 3 times with different stats, confirming this is a deliberate
template, not a one-off.

## S5 · The Confrontation Scene

**Skeleton:**
```
[Mid-scene opener: a sound, an action, a location.]

[Short narration line.]

[Quoted dialogue line — the prospect/other party speaks first.]

[Short narration reaction, 1 line.]

[Quoted dialogue — the response, reframing the moment.]

[1-2 lines: what this reveals/the lesson, stated plainly.]

[Final 1-2 line reframe, often symmetric to the opening line.]
```

**Rhythm rules:**
- Opens mid-action, never with setup or context ("Cold call. Ring ring." not "Let me tell you
  about a cold call I had.").
- Dialogue carries the post — narration lines are short and functional, never more than one
  clause.
- No bullets, no numbers. This is S2's cousin but scene-driven rather than rhythm-driven.

**Proven —** Josh Braun, "Cold call. Ring ring." (869 likes/124 comments) · "'Sell me this pen.'"
(721 likes/105 comments).

## How to pick one (for `post-structure-selection.md`)

- **S1** for synthesis/playbook content (F4, F12) — dense but scannable, high number density.
- **S2** for emotional/personal stories (F6) — never force a list into this shape.
- **S3** for tool-stack/system breakdowns (F2) — when "Tools used:" is a natural payoff.
- **S4** for benchmark/stat-comparison content (fits F8 Contrarian when the contrarian point is
  "X beats Y and here's the data").
- **S5** for a single dramatic moment illustrating one lesson (F6, sometimes F5).

**Never mix structures within one post.** Pick one, commit to its rhythm rules for the whole
body.
