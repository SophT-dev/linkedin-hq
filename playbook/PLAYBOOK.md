**7. ## PART 2 — THE 10 UNIVERSAL PRINCIPLES (cut across all 9)

1. **Give away secrets, sell implementation** (Hormozi, Enzo, Lieben, Illingworth).
2. **Systems over content** — build the engine, not the daily piece (Gray, Nowoslawski, Hormozi).
3. **Specificity is the moat** — one campaign / one week / one stat beats aggregated data (MrBeast, Lieben, Illingworth).
4. **Hook → open loop → escalate → payoff** — never resolve in line 2 (MrBeast, Lieben, Gray).
5. **Warm first, sell second** — content warms the room, comments warm the person, DM after warmth, pitch after DM (Lieben, Enzo, Nowoslawski, Jones).
6. **Multi-person brand = unfair advantage** — Taha + Sophiya multiplies, doesn't add (Lieben).
7. **Depth over virality** — 500 who trust you > 100K scrollers (Hormozi, Jones).
8. **Tools outlast posts** — an interactive tool earns authority daily (Patel, Enzo).
9. **Frameworks convert better than tips** — they get saved and reveal the need for help (Illingworth, Lieben).
10. **Engagement is a compounding asset** — comment before you post, reply after; early engagement sets reach (Lieben, Gray).

## PART 3 — MICHEL LIEBEN: THE OPERATING MANUAL

### The 7 laws (what he actually proved)
1. **Study great posts before writing a line.** You can't beat a standard you've never seen. Filter for min 300 likes, last 30 days; **creator-search beats topic-search** for a quality baseline.
2. **Visuals beat hooks — spend MORE time on the visual.** Great visuals get 2–10× engagement. A vertical **1080×1350** image fills the mobile screen and is a *second hook* (put a title inside it). A strong infographic can be the whole post. **Every post has a visual — no exceptions.**
3. **Repurpose your biggest hits — relentlessly.** His tool-stack infographic, reposted 5–6×, hits 100K+ impressions every time. Don't just repost — **rewrite slightly better** (swap vague claims for precise numbers). By month 5 you reuse winners and only write ~12 truly new posts to hit 20/month.
4. **Consistency is the whole game — trust-building before lead-gen.** His receipts: 30 posts, zero leads; post #31 (almost skipped) → 140K impressions, 25 meetings, 4 clients. LinkedIn also closes *existing* leads (prospect checks your profile, sees 50 posts of expertise) even when it generates none directly.
5. **Plan ahead with recurring formats** (tag them so batching is effortless): internal milestone breakdown · tool-category roundup.
6. **Go DEEP — don't fear the character limit.** Long in-depth posts get fewer likes but sign far more clients. **10K engaged followers can out-earn 100K passive ones.**
7. **Distribution = two manual levers beyond the algorithm:** (a) **connect with your ICP directly** so your posts surface to them; (b) **comments are a growth channel** — an insightful comment under a big creator often out-reaches your own post, and *commenting-only beat posting-only* for profile views. Build an **engagement list** (custom feed of chosen creators) and **reply to every comment on your post in the first 30 min** (early engagement decides 1,000 vs 10,000 reach).

### Anatomy of a great post (5 ingredients, in priority order)
1. **Proven angle / great idea** — the highest-leverage activity; spend disproportionate time on ideation (the MrBeast 10K-vs-1M-views point).
2. **Depth + real insight** — give away the whole thing (his tool-stack post gave the entire stack).
3. **Hook (first 2 lines)** — the **big-number formula**: lead with a credibility number that answers "why care?" (*"Lemlist crossed $34M ARR. Here's every tool they use to run their SaaS."*).
4. **Captivating 1080×1350 visual** — fills the screen, second hook inside, infographic can stand alone.
5. **Readable structure** — spacing, line breaks, light formatting. Never a wall of text.

### Employee-led / dual-brand mechanics (the WHO is in [STRATEGY.md](STRATEGY.md) Part 3)
Make posting a tracked, rewarded habit (he ran a quarterly competition; a $1K prize → $80K MRR) · make it **effortless** (a designer handles visuals so creators only write — for us, I supply angle + visual brief, you approve + lightly edit = our approval-first flow) · train to a visible standard · founder leads by example (Taha's profile most active) · it compounds (each success builds conviction for the next).

> **The one line:** *"Take pride in your consistency. View it as a trust-building mechanism before a lead-gen channel."* The viral post that brought 4 clients was the one he almost didn't publish, on post #31.

## PART 4 — CREATIVE FORMATS & FREE-TOOL IDEAS (nobody in this space is doing these)

1. **Live campaign scoreboard** — a public Next.js page with a real weekly stat ("847 sent, 76 replies, 29 positive, 6 calls"). No names, just live numbers. Proof-in-public beats any case study.
2. **Open-source workflow drops** — Sophiya publishes one real n8n workflow JSON/week as a lead magnet. Technical founders can't resist working code.
3. **"Cold email autopsy"** — line-by-line teardown of a real (blurred) email. Specific, visual, opinionated = save-bait.
4. **AI-personalized audit** — extend the spam-checker: paste your 3-step sequence → Claude returns a 10-point scored audit. We already have the infra.
5. **Build-in-public series** (Sophiya's lane) — "Building [X system] — Week N" with the actual n8n canvas, what broke, the download link.
6. **"What happened in our campaigns this week"** — Friday format, 3 raw bullets, no teaching. Specificity as proof.
7. **Interactive carousel** — a self-diagnosis mini-quiz ("what kind of cold-email problem do you have?"). Self-diagnosis always wins.
8. **LinkedIn → cold-email combo** — after any post >200 impressions, pull engaged companies → run them through outreach that references the post. It's a follow-up now, not cold (3–5× conversion).

**Free-tool note:** we work from Claude API · n8n · Notion API · the Apify free tier · GitHub Pages · Canva free · Loom free — **no new paid tools** (see [[feedback_resourcefulness_free_tools]]). Taplio/Scribe (swipe-file mining) and OpusClip (video) stay **deferred** until the free habit is proven / the YouTube phase lands.

## PART 5 — VOICE + HOOK PRINCIPLES (reference — SKILL.md is canonical)

**Voice rules live only in [`SKILL.md`](../.claude/skills/linkedin-batch/SKILL.md)** (lowercase, no em-dashes, banned words, the 4 authenticity tags, funnel + format mix). Do not restate them. The items below are *craft reference* the batch should check against:

- **Hook structure:** open loop (make not-reading painful) → line 2 raises the stakes → body builds tension → payoff at the end → final line opens the next loop.
- **Hook patterns by performance:** personal story (~4× avg) > specific number (~2.5×) > bold claim (2–3×) > contrarian (~2×) > open question (~1.5×).
- **The 4 authenticity tags** (≥1 each per batch): Numbers · Contrarian · BTS · Fresh Research.
- **Funnel check:** 40/40/20 TOFU/MOFU/BOFU per batch (5 posts → 2/2/1; 3 posts → 1/1/1).
- **Never:** theory without an operational example · close the loop in line 2 · generic tips ("5 ways to…") · a post without a specific number/name/moment · chase follower count over depth of trust.
- **The 5 recurring formats** to batch from: 📊 milestone breakdown · 🧰 tool roundup (most repeatable viral format) · 📬 real-reply teardown (fed by the email-mining pipeline) · 🧠 contrarian insight · 👤 personal story.

## PART 6 — PRE-BATCH CHECKLIST (run before every `/linkedin-batch`)

1. **Update WinsLog (5 min)** — new client wins, anonymized ("a SaaS founder in X space"): date · campaign · what we did · specific result · lesson.
2. **Star Intel / harvest signals (5 min)** — star 5–8 items that triggered a genuine reaction; note what surprised you or contradicts common wisdom. (Going forward the Brain queues these.)
3. **Set the funnel ratio** — how many posts → set TOFU/MOFU/BOFU counts; ensure ≥1 per authenticity tag.
4. **Set one creative intention** — pick ONE format you haven't tried (workflow drop, autopsy, build-in-public…). Just one.
5. **Run `/linkedin-batch`** with [STRATEGY.md](STRATEGY.md) + this doc as context; review every post against the hook structure before approving.
