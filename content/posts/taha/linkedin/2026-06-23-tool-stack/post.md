## Post copy (as published)

A few things about cold email tools I wish someone had just told me early on.

Sharing in case it saves you some money.

First, @Clay.

It's great, but it adds up fast (their entry plan is $167/mo right now).

The thing that helped us most: you can plug your own enrichment keys, like @Prospeo or @Findymail, straight into it.

Same data, you just pay the provider directly instead of through Clay. Works out a lot cheaper.

Second, trim your list down to your actual ICP before you start enriching, not after.

Sounds obvious, but it's so easy to quietly burn money enriching people who were never a fit anyway.

Third, on sending.

That advice about ramping up to 50 a day per inbox? Just be a little careful.

Around 35 a day keeps your inboxes healthy for the long run. Slow and steady really does win this one.

And honestly, you don't need a fancy setup to start.

Something like @Smartlead ($39/mo) plus a few cheap inboxes is plenty to book real meetings.

You can add Clay later, when you're scaling and it actually earns its spot.
