# ⚡ 1. The Philosophy

Before you touch a single tool, understand how Eric Nowoslawski actually thinks about cold email. His whole approach rests on a small set of stubborn beliefs: **research beats volume**, a **real signal beats generic AI personalization**, and **fundamentals plus a great offer** do the heavy lifting. Everything else in this guide is just plumbing that serves those ideas.

### Fundamentals win, not genius

Eric is blunt about where results actually come from. When he told a friend the real reason his agency gets clients results, it wasn't clever tricks:

> "I used to think my agency was getting people results because I was a genius, but then I realized it's because we follow the fundamentals and our clients have great offers."

He doubles down: *"We just do things to give them the straightest path to success."* That "straightest path" is a stack of unglamorous fundamentals done right. In that same post he names his own defaults: **inboxes** from Alphoric and Hypertide.io, **email validation** double-verified across TryKitt.ai, Prospeo.io, Icypeas, and Instantly.ai's verification, **sending** on Smartlead, and **leads filtered and AI cleaned by Clay**. No magic. Just clean inputs.

He breaks the whole game into three levers you have to nail:

> "Sending, List Building, and Campaign Strategy. Nail those, and you'll win."

- **Sending** — use a real outbound platform (Smartlead.ai or Instantly.ai), send from Google or Outlook inboxes, buy .com or .co domains (from Porkbun or Dynadot), spin up inboxes via Zapmail.ai or Hypertide.io, and warm them up properly.
- **List Building** — understand most list platforms are just scraping LinkedIn and guessing emails; pick your sources deliberately.
- **Campaign Strategy** — use a proven format you iterate on weekly, and if a campaign fails, adjust your template and list filters fast (usually after 1,000 contacts).

> 📌 Proof this scales: *"In 2025, we launched 1,517 campaigns and generated over 60k positive responses across all of our customers."* When Eric had 30 Claude sub-agents analyze that database, one of the loudest findings was #9: *"Generic AI personalization killed reply rates"* — the fundamentals held across 1,500+ campaigns.

### Signal over generic AI personalization

Eric changed his mind publicly on AI first lines, and the reversal is a core part of his philosophy now:

> "The tests are in and we are not allowing generic AI personalizations based on company descriptions as first lines anymore."

He used to see lift from AI-generated first lines scraped off company websites. The data flipped. His reasoning: *"Sophisticated buyers recognize generic AI personalization instantly which hurts your message."* They've seen "I noticed you're focused on helping local businesses..." a thousand times.

His replacement rule is a clean fork:

1. **If you have a real signal** — use it. Something specific, custom-scraped, tied directly to their problem and your value prop.
2. **If you don't have a signal** — treat cold email like a banner ad. Put the offer in the preview text, make it punchy, and skip the fake personalization. As he puts it, the subject line plus preview text is *"your click opportunity."*

### Research you can't copy

The deepest belief: your best strategy can't be lifted from anyone's post, because it comes from actually looking at the prospect. His test for good research is a single question:

> "what would you send if you had 10 minutes to research a prospect and how would the research change your message."

He asks new customers that exact question, and notes *"very often people don't have a good answer for us."* That gap is the whole opportunity.

> 💡 Fundamentals plus a great offer do the winning; a real researched signal beats generic AI personalization every time — and if you have no signal, sell the offer in the preview text instead of faking one.
