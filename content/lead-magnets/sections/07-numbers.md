# 📊 7. The Numbers He's Shared

Eric backs his advice with real figures from running an outbound agency for four years. Every number below is pulled straight from one of his posts — no benchmarks invented, no rounding up.

### Response & conversion benchmarks

- **1 positive response per 140 leads uploaded** *(Jan 17, 2025)* — from his "first-time CEO" trigger campaign, which filters for people who've never held their current role before. He notes the trigger "can only be found in Clay and costs just **$.0005 per row**."

- **1 positive response for every 300 people** *(Feb 6, 2026)* — his *old* scaling benchmark. "3 years ago, I said a campaign was successful and should be scaled if it was getting 1 positive response for every 300 people you reach out to. The numbers have moved for sure."

### Cost-per-step economics

- **Claygent research: under $0.01 per company; copy: less than $0.001** *(Jan 15, 2025)* — the cost of using 4o mini to find a case study on a prospect's website and write the injected line. "It's usually under .01 per prospective company and then less than .001 to write the messaging we need to inject into the copywriting."

### Scale of the operation

- **23M+ valid emails cached** *(Dec 17, 2025)* — the result of saving every email they find in Supabase and checking the cache before buying new data. "Constantly requerying instead of repurchasing... Massive cost savings."

- **8 customers sending over 25k emails/day — one over 100k/day** *(Dec 5, 2025)* — kept full automatically via Clay + Supabase. That 100k/day sales team "gets about 125ish leads per day and their TAM has 6M people in it." He adds: "I'm processing **1.8M leads** for a client right now actually."

- **1,517 campaigns launched in 2025, 60k+ positive responses** *(Feb 6, 2026)* — across all customers. "In 2025, we launched 1,517 campaigns and generated over 60k positive responses across all of our customers." He then threw **30 Claude sub agents** at the database to extract 9 lessons (e.g. "70-90 words was optimal for email length").

### The build

- **2M lines of code in 45 days of Claude Code** *(Apr 21, 2026)* — how heavily he's leaned into building his own tooling. "I've been working daily in Claude code since January. With 45 days of usage, I had written 2M lines of code to my computer." This is what produced the 29-skill (later 30) repo he gave away.

> 💡 The through-line in Eric's numbers: cheap triggers (fractions of a cent per row), short copy, cached data, and automation at massive scale — the response rate comes from the *system*, not from spending more per lead.
