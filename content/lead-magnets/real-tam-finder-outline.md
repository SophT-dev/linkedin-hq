## Section structure

1. **What TAM actually means (and why your number is probably wrong)**
   Beginner-level. TAM defined in plain English. The common mistake: quoting a market-research-report TAM instead of a reachable TAM. Introduce TAM vs. SAM vs. SOM.

2. **How to calculate the TAM you can actually reach**
   The SAM/SOM narrowing math. The funnel math backward from a revenue goal (customers needed → close rate → opportunities → meeting rate → meetings → reply rate → sends). Where bounce rate and enrichment match rate fit into the honest version of this math.

3. **The general-purpose toolkit (works for any industry)**
   Apollo, Prospeo, Apify (Leads Finder, HarvestAPI, Compass Google Maps Scraper, B2B Lead Gen Suite), the niche-directory tiers (Crunchbase, G2, Clutch / Capterra, GetApp, Software Advice, Trustpilot, BBB / regional directories), Google Maps for local.

4. **Industry-by-industry source map** — one subsection each, with real named tools + links:
   - SaaS / software
   - Agencies & professional services
   - E-commerce / DTC brands
   - Local / brick-and-mortar businesses
   - Recruitment / staffing
   - Real estate
   - Healthcare / medical practices
   - Manufacturing / industrial
   - B2B financial services

5. **How to stack sources (the waterfall)**
   Why one source caps out around 50-60% match rate and stacking 2-3 sources gets you to 85-95%. How to sequence it (broad database → technographic/firmographic filter → niche directory → verify/clean).

6. **Quick-start checklist**
   A short "do this today" list: pick your industry section, pull 100 names from source #1, run through source #2 for enrichment, check bounce rate before sending.
