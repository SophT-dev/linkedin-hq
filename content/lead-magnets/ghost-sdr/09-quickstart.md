# Quick-Start: Your First Self-Running Campaign in 7 Steps

**Reading is not building. This page turns everything in the kit into one first campaign you can start now. Do not try to run the whole system at once. Run one small, honest campaign end to end, read the two numbers that matter, and decide. Work down the list.**

### The checklist

1. **Pick one signal and one segment.**
   - [ ] Choose a single trigger you can actually detect (a launch, a funding event, a new hire, a public post on a topic you solve).
   - [ ] Choose one narrow segment that this signal fits. One. Not five.

2. **Set up domains and inboxes, and start warming.**
   - [ ] Register sending domains separate from your main domain.
   - [ ] Create a small number of inboxes on them.
   - [ ] Start warmup now, before you write a word of copy. Warmup takes time, so it must run in the background while you do the rest.

3. **Build a small verified list.**
   - [ ] Source contacts for your one segment.
   - [ ] Run a waterfall: try one email-finding provider, then fall back to another on the misses.
   - [ ] For catch-all domains in your segment, verify with a dedicated catch-all check before you trust the address.
   - [ ] Gate and dedupe BEFORE paid enrichment (the Data-Minimum Gate).

4. **Write one strong email plus a 4-touch follow-up.**
   - [ ] Lead with the offer, not with "I." One clear reason for this segment to reply.
   - [ ] Reference the signal so it reads as human, not bulk.
   - [ ] Run the human second pass: kill em dashes, buzzwords, and the robotic shape.
   - [ ] Write a 4-touch follow-up sequence. Most replies come from follow-ups, not the first email.

5. **Route each job to the right model tier.**
   - [ ] Cheap classifier for gating and segment checks.
   - [ ] Mid-tier generator for first lines and follow-ups.
   - [ ] Expensive deep-research reserved for hand-picked accounts only, never the whole list.

6. **Launch small and read only reply and bounce.**
   - [ ] Start with a small daily volume. Do not blast.
   - [ ] Track only reply rate and bounce rate. Ignore opens and platform scores.
   - [ ] If bounce climbs past your ceiling, stop and fix deliverability before anything else.

7. **At day 21, decide kill or scale.**
   - [ ] Read your reply rate against your floor.
   - [ ] Above the floor with clean bounce: scale volume slowly.
   - [ ] Below the floor: kill it, change ONE variable (segment, offer, or signal), and rerun.

> 💡 If you only remember one thing: offer plus segment plus deliverability beat clever copy every time. A plain email to the right person from an inbox that lands will out-reply a brilliant email to the wrong person from a burnt domain. Get those three right first. Polish the words last.

Work this list once and you have done the thing most people only read about: a real campaign, running on the right tiers, judged on the only two numbers that tell the truth.
