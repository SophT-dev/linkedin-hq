Cold email breaks in the same five places for almost everyone: a soft list nobody cleaned, random timing, a robotic first line, follow-ups that just say "bumping this," and no record of what actually worked. ## The 10 skills

**1. ICP Definer** — Turns a fuzzy "we sell to B2B founders" into a filterable profile: exact titles, company size, the buying trigger, and hard disqualifiers. *Why it matters:* personalization in 2026 is binary — generic first-name/company tokens reply around **3%**, genuine business-context relevance jumps to **15-30%**, with almost no middle. A tight ICP is what makes that relevance possible, which is why every other skill reads its output first.

**2. List Auditor** — Scores a raw list against your ICP *before* you pay to verify it, flags role accounts and catch-alls, and splits it into keep / check / drop. *Why it matters:* this is where bounce gets decided. A list nobody cleans routinely runs **6.5%+ bounce**, and every bounce hurts the whole domain's reputation. Cleaning against the ICP first is the cheapest place to prevent it.

**3. Account Researcher** — Briefs one company in five lines: what they sell, where they're likely stuck in your area, and the one specific detail worth opening with. *Why it matters:* it manufactures the specific business context that separates a 3% reply from a 15-30% one — and it's the raw material the Opener Writer needs to do its job.

**4. "Why Now" Finder** — Finds the timing signal that makes *this* the right week: funding, a relevant new hire, a telling job post, a tech switch, a new location. *Why it matters:* the modern doctrine is to email buyers who already have a reason to want you. A trigger turns a name into a reason to write — and decides your sending order, freshest trigger first.

**5. Opener Writer** — Writes the first line and only the first line: **signal → implication → proof**, with hard rules against deletion-triggering phrases. *Why it matters:* the naked signal ("I saw you raised a Series A") is pattern-matched as scraped outreach instantly now, and greetings like "I hope this finds you well" are dead on arrival. The data point isn't the hook — the *implication* is.

**6. Sequence Builder** — Designs 4-7 follow-ups where each touch adds a new angle or proof point, never "just checking in." *Why it matters:* about **58% of replies come from email one, 42% from the follow-ups** — quitting after one send leaves nearly half your results behind. Sweet spot: **4-7 touches, 3-4 days apart**, each in the 50-125 word band, CTA escalating from soft to direct.

**7. Deliverability Sentinel** — The pre-send gate and the daily alarm: warmup done, per-inbox volume sane, copy plain, bounce watched. *Why it matters:* the best copy does nothing from spam. The consensus rules — **20-25 sends per inbox per day** (scale by adding inboxes, never raising the ceiling), SPF/DKIM/DMARC on every domain, **2-4 week warmup** — and the alarm: bounce past **2% = slow**, past **5% = reputation emergency**.

**8. Reply Triager** — Sorts replies into interested / not now / wrong person / objection, drafts warm responses, and shows you the batch — but **never sends**. *Why it matters:* the confirm gate is the load-bearing part. You get automation's speed with a human's safety, so a wrong message never reaches a real buyer, and warm deals don't rot in an untriaged inbox.

**9. Campaign Scoreboard** — Pulls the two numbers that matter and says plainly what to cut and what to feed — ignoring open rate on purpose. *Why it matters:* **reply rate** averages **1-5%** (10%+ is good); **positive reply rate** averages **0.1-0.5%** (1-3% is good) and is the real scoreboard number. Open tracking is noise in 2026 and can hurt placement — leave it out.

**10. Win Logger** — Captures a proof point the moment it lands — a booked call, a strong reply, a closed deal — and files the one reusable stat. *Why it matters:* specific proof is exactly what pushes replies from generic toward relevant. "We help companies grow" is a claim; "recovered 40+ booking calls a month for a 25-tech HVAC shop" is proof. A logged win today is next month's best first line — that's the flywheel.

## Start with these two

You don't have to build all ten at once.

> **① ICP Definer** — because every other skill reads its output. A loose ICP silently weakens the list, the research, the opener, and the score.
>

Run a campaign through those two, then add the next once the first has earned its place.

## FAQ

**Do I need to be technical?** No. If you can copy a folder, you can install it — drop ten folders into `~/.claude/skills/`, restart Claude, done. Full step-by-step (and a no-git ZIP option) is in the repo's `INSTALL.md`.

**Do I need a new paid tool?** No. The skills run inside Claude and describe processes you already do. They reference standard categories — a sending platform, a verifier, a warmup tool — but add zero new subscriptions.

**Will it send emails automatically?** No, and that's deliberate. The Reply Triager never sends; it drafts and shows you the batch, and you keep the button. These skills make you fast and consistent — they don't take the wheel.

**Are the numbers real?** Yes. Every benchmark — 20-25 sends/inbox/day, the 2% bounce alarm, 4-7 touch sequences, positive-reply-rate scoring, the 3% vs. 15-30% personalization gap — is 2026 cold-email consensus. Each skill file cites the benchmark behind its rule.

**Can I change the rules?** Absolutely — they're plain files you own. Edit the banned phrases, the bounce threshold, the touch count. Once it's in your skills folder, it's yours.
