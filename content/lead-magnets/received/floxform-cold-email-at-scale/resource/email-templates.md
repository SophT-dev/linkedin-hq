Intro line (verbatim):
> Skeletons, not finished copy. Rewrite every word in your own voice, kill the em dashes, and make sure anything claiming a client, result, or guarantee is real. These six come from a cold-email operator who credits them with 10,000+ booked leads (their claim, not ours):

---

### 1. Lead magnet + same-industry case study
```
{{first_name}}, made a {{lead magnet}} for {{company}} on the {{mechanism}} we used to get {{client in same industry}} to {{result}} in {{timeframe}}. {{CTA}}?
```

### 2. One-line free-work offer
```
{{first_name}}, interested in {{free work / frontend offer}} for {{company}}?

P.S. {{social proof}}
```

### 3. Guarantee / risk reversal
```
{{first_name}}, interested in {{dream result}} with {{mechanism}} for {{company}} in the next {{timeframe}}?

Our {{mechanism}} guarantees {{dream result}} in {{timeframe}} or {{risk reversal}}. {{CTA}}?
```

### 4. Pain question, then solution
```
{{first_name}}, {{question about a pain point}}?

Our {{solution}} {{how it fixes it}} so you {{end result}}.

{{interest-based CTA}}

P.S. {{social proof}}
```

### 5. Signal touchpoint, pain, quick fix
```
{{first_name}}, {{relevant touchpoint}}.

Noticed {{relevant pain point}}.

Interested in {{quick fix}}?
```

### 6. Touchpoint + unique market insight
```
{{first_name}}, {{question about a relevant touchpoint}}.

{{unique market insight tied to it}}.

{{CTA to implement the insight}}
```
