# Price Based on Value, Not Cost

**Source:** unknown (sub-page inside 'The Ultimate Outbound Sales Playbook') — https://www.notion.so/Price-Based-on-Value-Not-Cost-21d0435e95c78075a956c5ff57d66b63?pvs=21
**Topic:** offer-creation · **Type:** notion-page

## Key takeaways
- Price on perceived worth to the client, not your cost: 'If your offer delivers $10,000 in value, charging $2,000 should feel like a steal.'
- Raising price increases perceived value AND client behavior: clients take the work more seriously, show up, do the work, and respect the process because 'price signals quality.'
- Explicit anti-race-to-the-bottom stance: 'Don't race to the bottom. Price like your solution works. Then make sure it actually does.'

## Excerpt
People don't buy based on what something costs you — they buy based on what it's worth to them. That's the game. Your price should make people pause — but not walk away. It should match the transformation you're promising. Charging more doesn't just increase your revenue — it increases the perceived value. When you raise your price: clients take your work more seriously, they show up and stay engaged, they respect your process, they assume it's premium because price signals quality. And with more revenue, you can afford to deliver an even better experience. Don't race to the bottom. Price like your solution works. Then make sure it actually does.
