## Deliverability Foundation
**Why it matters:** "If your emails don't land in the inbox, nothing else matters."

**Best practices:**
1. Use Google Workspace senders — highest inbox placement
2. Send conservative volumes — 20 cold emails/day max per inbox
3. Keep warm-up always on — maintains sender reputation
4. Age domains for SEG-heavy lists — targeting Microsoft, Barracuda, Proofpoint, Mimecast users
5. Test before scaling — multiple ESP combinations

## Lead Quality Strategy — Directory Scraping Approach
- Extract company names from niche directories (rather than relying on overused Apollo/ZoomInfo lists)
- Use Clearbit in Clay to convert company names to domains
- Reference the source in the first line: "I saw you on the [Directory] list"

## Decision Maker Outreach
**Generic emails work:** "For smaller businesses, these inboxes are often monitored by decision makers."
**Public email addresses:** Gmail/Yahoo addresses listed on websites are fair game for business inquiries.

## Personalization Without LinkedIn (four tactics)
- Build relevant lists (niche directories)
- Reference where you found them (directory/association)
- Differentiate your approach (specificity over volume)
- Company-level personalization (website, reviews, SEMrush data)

## Follow-Up Strategy
**The Two-Touch Rule:** "One email, maybe two touches at most. If they don't respond, we let them go and re-engage a couple months later."
**Re-engagement:** Wait 60–90 days with a fresh angle and messaging.

## Key Metrics
| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| PCPL (Prospects Contacted Per Lead) | 500 or less |
| Reply rate minimum | 2% |
| Show-up rate goal | 70% |

Quote: "Stop tracking open rates. They don't work anymore, period."

## No-Show Solutions
1. Call immediately upon positive reply
2. Send text confirmation before the call
3. Call at the scheduled appointment time (don't wait around on Zoom)
