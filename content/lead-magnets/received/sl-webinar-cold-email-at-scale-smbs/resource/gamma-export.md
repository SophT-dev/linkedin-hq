## Slide 3 — Deliverability: Your Foundation for Success

### Why Deliverability Matters
If your emails don't land in the inbox, nothing else matters.

Most SMB sales teams burn through domains because they're skipping infrastructure setup or sending too aggressively. The solution isn't complicated, but it requires discipline.

**Segment lists by ESP type**
- Using Clay: Resolve DNS > Identify recipient ESP and contacts using SEGs

> Pro Tip: Always keep email warm-up running. It's not optional. It's essential for maintaining sender reputation.

**Use Google Inboxes**
For most SMBs, using Google Workspace senders is your best bet. Google-to-Google emails have the highest inbox placement rates. Microsoft and shared Azure setups? Skip them unless you're trying to scale and every 10% improvement matters.

**Use Aged Domains**
If your target list uses Microsoft, Barracuda, Proofpoint, Mimecast, or other Secure Email Gateways (SEGs), domain age becomes critical. These systems heavily weight domain age alongside proper DNS records and non-phishing copy.

## Slide 4 — Deliverability Best Practices
1. **Use Google Workspace Senders** — Highest inbox placement for SMB outreach. This is your default choice unless you have a reason to go elsewhere.
2. **Send Conservative Volumes** — Limit yourself to 20 cold emails per day, per inbox. More than this and you risk triggering spam filters.
3. **Keep Warm-Up Always On** — Never turn off warm-up. It maintains your sender reputation and keeps you in good standing with email providers.
4. **Age Domains for SEG-Heavy Lists** — If your list consists of businesses using Microsoft or SEGs, age your domains and warm them up for longer periods before sending.
5. **Test Before Scaling** — Run tests: Google-to-Google, Google-to-Outlook, Outlook-to-Outlook, Outlook-to-Google. See which gives you the best inbox placement, then double down.

## Slide 5 — Lead Quality: Stop Relying on Apollo or Zoominfo
Apollo/ZoomInfo/Seamless.ai lists are: overused · misclassified contacts · often outdated.

If you're only using Apollo, ZoomInfo, or Seamless.ai to build lists, you're setting yourself up for failure. These databases are convenient, but they're overused. Your prospects are bombarded with emails from everyone using the same filters and keywords. The result? Terrible reply rates and wasted time.

### The Directory Scraping Strategy
Instead, find niche-specific directories. Use ChatGPT to identify directories for your target industry. For example, if you're targeting dentists, you might find the U.S. Association of Clear Aligners directory. This gives you a list of businesses in a specific subtype, allowing for highly relevant outreach.

When you scrape directories, you can reference where you found them in your email. A first line like "I saw you on the Top 100 Dentists list from the U.S. Association of Clear Aligners" immediately captures attention because prospects think you have something nice to say about them, or at minimum, they're curious.

## Slide 7 — Reaching Decision Makers: Generic Emails Work

### Why Generic Emails Matter
Many people dismiss info@, contact@, or service@ email addresses. Big mistake. For smaller businesses, these inboxes are often monitored by decision makers. A clinic with three doctors? The head doctor probably still checks the main inbox.

Service inboxes reply to all emails, which is actually good for your deliverability. Even if you don't get a sale, the engagement helps your sender reputation.

### Public Email Addresses Are Fair Game
Gmail and Yahoo addresses listed on websites or social profiles? Absolutely reach out. If an email is publicly available for business inquiries, there's nothing wrong with sending a cold email to it.

> Legal Note: Emails made publicly available on websites or social media are fair game for business outreach. You're not violating any laws by contacting them.

## Slide 8 — Relevance Without LinkedIn Signals
Traditional B2B research tactics—LinkedIn activity, hiring signals, intent data—don't work with SMBs. Most small business owners aren't active on LinkedIn, don't post updates, and don't hire publicly. So how do you create relevance?

- **Build Relevant Lists** — Start with niche directories so your targeting is precise from the beginning. This makes your outreach inherently more relevant.
- **Reference Where You Found Them** — Mention the directory, association, or list you found them on. This creates instant credibility and curiosity.
- **Differentiate Your Approach** — Anticipate what other emails they're receiving and make yours different. Stand out through specificity, not volume.
- **Company-Level Personalization** — Look at their website, past work, customer reviews, or SEMrush data. Personalize based on the company, not the individual.

At the end of the day, SMB outreach boils down to three things: your offer, your social proof, and your ability to speak their language. Your copy needs to be short, direct, and show you understand what they do.

## Slide 9 — Follow-Up Strategy: Less Is More

### The Two-Touch Rule
Our sequences are intentionally short: one email, maybe two touches at most. If they don't respond, we let them go and re-engage a couple months later with completely different messaging.

This performs better than sending four, five, or six follow-ups. Why? Because long sequences annoy prospects and increase your chances of being reported as spam. With SMBs, you're often reaching busy owners who don't appreciate being hounded.

**When to Re-Engage** — Wait 60-90 days, then hit them again with a fresh angle. New offer, different pain point, updated social proof. This approach respects their time and maintains your sender reputation.

## Slide 10 — Tracking the Right Metrics
Stop tracking open rates. They don't work anymore, period. If you're still doing it, let this be the last time. Here are the metrics that actually matter for SMB cold email:

- **PCPL Target** — Prospects Contacted Per Lead (or Positive Response). Your North Star metric. Under 500 means you're doing well.
- **Reply Rate Minimum** — Less than 2% reply rate means your emails are going to spam. Switch domains and change copy immediately.
- **Show-Up Rate Goal** — Your booked meetings should show at this rate or higher. If not, your follow-up process needs work.

### What PCPL Really Means
Your PCPL should be 500 or less—meaning one positive response for every 500 people contacted. Optimize this number as low as possible. Everything else (reply rate, deliverability, list quality) contributes to improving your PCPL.

Close rate will vary based on your offer, so there's no universal benchmark. But if you nail the metrics above, your close rate will take care of itself.

## Slide 11 — Fixing No-Shows: Pick Up the Phone
SMB prospects don't show up to Zoom calls for two reasons: they're busy in the field (doctors, service contractors, etc.), or they simply forgot. You can't fix the first one by waiting on Zoom. Here's what works:

1. **Call as Soon as They Respond** — When you get a positive reply, have someone from your team call them immediately to book the appointment right then. Strike while the iron is hot.
2. **Send a Text Before the Call** — Confirm appointments via text message. SMB owners live on their phones - use that to your advantage.
3. **Call Them at Appointment Time** — Instead of waiting on Zoom, pick up the phone and call the prospect when the meeting is scheduled. Catch them wherever they are.

> Golden Rule: Use your phone more than your computer if you want to do business with SMBs. They're not sitting at desks—they're out running their businesses.

## Slide 12 — Key Takeaways: Your SMB Cold Email Playbook (7 items — now fully itemized)
1. **Deliverability First** — Use Google Workspace, send 20 emails/day max, keep warm-up on, and age domains for SEG-heavy lists.
2. **Scrape Niche Directories** — Stop relying on Apollo and ZoomInfo. Find directories, scrape them, convert names to domains with Clay.
3. **Generic Emails Work** — Don't dismiss info@ or Gmail addresses. For SMBs, decision makers often monitor these inboxes.
4. **Company-Level Personalization** — Skip LinkedIn stalking. Personalize based on their website, reviews, SEMrush data, or directory listing.
5. **Two Touches Max** — Short sequences prevent spam reports. Re-engage in 60-90 days with fresh messaging.
6. **Track PCPL, Not Opens** — Your North Star is Prospects Contacted Per Lead. Aim for 500 or less, and ignore open rates entirely.
7. **Call, Don't Wait** — Fix no-shows by calling prospects at appointment time. Use your phone more than your computer.

These strategies are practical, cost-effective, and proven to work for SMB cold email. Implement them systematically, track the right metrics, and you'll see your PCPL drop and your pipeline grow.
