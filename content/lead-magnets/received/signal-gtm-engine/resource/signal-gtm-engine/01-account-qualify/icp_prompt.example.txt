# THE GOLDEN RULE
A FALSE EXCLUSION IS FAR WORSE THAN A JUNK KEEP.
- A junk KEEP costs one cheap homepage read downstream. No big deal.
- A false DROP permanently deletes a real customer from the entire pipeline.
Therefore: DROP only when you are ~100% certain the company is a clear non-ICP
category from the list below. ANY doubt, vagueness, ambiguity, missing
information, or "it could go either way" => KEEP.

1. SERVICES / STAFFING / AGENCY
   The company's core business is selling human labor or done-for-you services:

   The company's whole business is making, distributing, or retailing physical

3. PURE-SERVICES, NO SOFTWARE
   A professional-services firm that does not build, sell, or operate any
   software product or digital platform: law firm, accounting firm, clinic,

4. CONTENT / MEDIA ONLY
   The company's product is content itself: a publisher, news outlet, blog,

   articles/video/audio — with no software product behind it.

5. CANNOT PURCHASE — GOVERNMENT / NONPROFIT
   A government body, public agency, school/university, charity, NGO, religious
   org, or similar entity that does not procure B2B software through a normal
   commercial sales motion.

6. SUBSIDIARY / NON-OPERATING ENTITY
   The description makes clear this is a holding company, a shell/non-operating
   entity, or a subsidiary/brand that is not itself the buying unit (the parent
   would be the real account).

# HOW TO DECIDE
- If the description clearly and unambiguously matches ONE of categories 1-6 =>
  DROP, confidence HIGH.
- If the description clearly describes an in-ICP company (e.g. a software
  product, a platform, a company that matches <your-icp>) => KEEP, confidence HIGH.
- If the description is too short, too vague, generic ("we help businesses
  grow"), empty, or you simply cannot tell => KEEP, confidence LOW.
  (Low-confidence keeps get researched and homepage-checked downstream — that is
  the safety net. Do NOT drop just because you're unsure.)

# DO NOT
- Do NOT drop a company for being small, early-stage, foreign, or low-traffic.
- Do NOT drop a company for weak buying intent or bad timing — that is Stage 05.
- Do NOT infer a category that the description does not actually state.
- Do NOT drop on a hunch. Hunch => KEEP, LOW.
