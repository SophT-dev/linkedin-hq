# Thought Leadership Carousel

## Purpose

Generate a multi-slide LinkedIn carousel that builds credibility and earns attention through a genuine point of view. Thought leadership carousels perform best when they challenge a common assumption, share a counterintuitive framework, or make a prediction backed by reasoning. Use this when you want to grow an audience around a specific idea, not promote a product.

## Prompt

```
You are a LinkedIn content strategist and ghostwriter. Write a 10-slide thought leadership carousel for the following brief.

Author name and title: [NAME] — [JOB TITLE OR AREA OF EXPERTISE]
Core idea or argument: [THE MAIN POINT YOU WANT TO MAKE IN ONE SENTENCE]
The conventional wisdom you are challenging or expanding: [WHAT MOST PEOPLE BELIEVE THAT YOU DISAGREE WITH OR WANT TO NUANCE]
Your evidence or reasoning: [DATA, EXPERIENCE, PATTERN YOU HAVE OBSERVED — 3-5 POINTS]
Target audience: [WHO SHOULD STOP SCROLLING WHEN THEY SEE SLIDE 1]
Tone: [CHOOSE: direct and sharp / warm and conversational / analytical / contrarian]
One thing you want readers to take away: [THE SINGLE MOST IMPORTANT IDEA]

Write 10 slides. For each slide, write:
- Slide number
- Headline (under 8 words, designed to create curiosity or tension)
- Body copy (2-5 short lines or bullets — as if written on a real slide)
- Visual direction (one sentence describing what the slide should look like — layout, color mood, whether it is text-only or uses a diagram)

Slide structure:
1. Hook — a bold claim or question that makes the audience stop
2. Setup — the context, the common belief, what most people think
3-8. The argument — one point per slide, building toward the conclusion
9. Synthesis — the full framework or takeaway in one clear visual or statement
10. CTA — a specific action (follow, comment, share, DM, visit a link)

Rules:
- No jargon without a plain-English definition
- Slide 1 must work as a standalone image even without reading the others
- Each slide body should be readable in under 5 seconds
- The CTA must be specific — "follow me" alone is not enough
```

## Example Output Description

Ten slide briefs, each with a bold headline. Slide 1 opens with a claim like "Most [X] strategies fail for the same reason." Middle slides each contain 3-4 lines making one point with a concrete example or data point. Slide 9 presents a named framework or diagram description. Slide 10 ends with a question to the audience and a follow prompt.
