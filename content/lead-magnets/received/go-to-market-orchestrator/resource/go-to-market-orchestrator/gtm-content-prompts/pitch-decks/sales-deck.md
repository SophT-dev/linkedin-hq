# Sales Pitch Deck — 5 Slides

## Purpose

Generate a tight 5-slide sales deck for use in discovery or demo calls. This deck is not for investors — it is for a buyer who already knows they have a problem and needs to understand quickly whether your product solves it and what it costs.

## Prompt

```
You are a sales enablement writer. Write a 5-slide sales pitch deck for the following product.

Company name: [COMPANY NAME]
Product name: [PRODUCT NAME]
What it does in one sentence: [DESCRIPTION]
Target buyer: [JOB TITLE AND COMPANY TYPE OF THE PERSON RECEIVING THIS DECK]
Primary pain this buyer has: [THE SPECIFIC BUSINESS PROBLEM]
Top 3 capabilities: [CAPABILITY 1], [CAPABILITY 2], [CAPABILITY 3]
Proof point or social proof: [A CUSTOMER NAME, METRIC, OR QUOTE — OR "[INSERT PROOF]"]
Pricing model: [HOW IT IS PRICED — OR "CUSTOM / CONTACT US"]
Call to action: [WHAT YOU WANT THEM TO DO NEXT — TRIAL, DEMO, CONTRACT, ETC.]

For each slide, write:
- Slide title (short, outcome-oriented)
- Headline sentence (the one thing this slide must land)
- Body content: 3-5 bullets or a short structured layout
- Presenter note: what to say, what question to ask the buyer to drive engagement

Slides to include:
1. Opening — who you are, what you do, why this buyer should care
2. The problem — frame their pain in their language, make it feel urgent
3. The solution — your product's core workflow, specific to their role
4. Proof — results achieved by similar companies, with numbers
5. Next step — clear CTA, what happens in the next 48 hours

Write in second person where possible ("your team," "your pipeline"). Avoid technical jargon unless the target buyer is technical. Keep each bullet under 12 words.
```

## Example Output Description

A lean document with five sections. The opening slide has a one-line company descriptor and a buyer-specific headline. The problem slide uses the buyer's own language and frames inaction as costly. The solution slide describes a three-step workflow with a concrete example. The proof slide includes a named or anonymized customer with a before/after metric. The next step slide specifies a single action, a timeline, and a contact.
