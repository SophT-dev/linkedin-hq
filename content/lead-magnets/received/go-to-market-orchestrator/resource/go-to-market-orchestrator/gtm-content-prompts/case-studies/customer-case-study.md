# Customer Success Case Study

## Purpose

Generate a full-length customer success case study suitable for publishing on a website, attaching to a proposal, or sharing with prospects during a sales cycle. This is the long-form version — more detailed than the one-pager, structured for readers who want to understand the full context before making a decision.

## Prompt

```
You are a B2B content writer specializing in customer success stories. Write a full-length case study based on the following inputs.

Customer name or label: [COMPANY NAME — OR "A HEALTHCARE TECHNOLOGY COMPANY"]
Customer description: [INDUSTRY, COMPANY SIZE, GEOGRAPHY, WHAT THEY DO]
Your company name: [NAME]
Your product or service: [WHAT WAS USED OR IMPLEMENTED]
The situation before (the challenge): [DESCRIBE IN DETAIL — WHAT WAS BROKEN, SLOW, OR MISSING, AND WHAT IT COST THEM]
Why this problem mattered: [BUSINESS IMPACT — REVENUE, EFFICIENCY, RISK, CUSTOMER EXPERIENCE, ETC.]
How they found you or why they chose you: [THE BUYING STORY — WHAT THEY EVALUATED, WHAT TIPPED IT]
What was implemented (the solution): [DESCRIBE THE APPROACH, TIMELINE, AND ANY INTEGRATION OR ONBOARDING STEPS]
Key milestones during implementation: [FIRST VALUE DATE, FULL ROLLOUT DATE, ETC.]
Results — quantified: [LIST ALL METRICS WITH BEFORE/AFTER OR ABSOLUTE NUMBERS]
Results — qualitative: [HOW THE TEAM FEELS, WHAT IS EASIER, WHAT IS NOW POSSIBLE]
Customer quote 1 (from the main contact): [QUOTE OR "[INSERT QUOTE]"]
Customer quote 2 (optional, from another stakeholder): [QUOTE OR OMIT]
What they plan to do next: [EXPANSION, NEW USE CASE, RENEWAL, ETC.]

Structure the case study as follows:
1. Headline — results-led, specific (e.g., "How [Customer] cut [metric] by [X%] in [timeframe]")
2. Subheadline — one sentence expanding on the headline
3. Customer snapshot — a 4-field info box: industry, company size, location, use case
4. The Challenge — two to three paragraphs describing the problem in detail
5. Why [Your Company] — one to two paragraphs on the buying decision
6. The Solution — two to three paragraphs on what was implemented and how
7. The Results — a stat block (3-5 metrics) followed by two paragraphs of narrative
8. In Their Words — the primary quote, prominently displayed
9. What Is Next — one paragraph on the ongoing relationship or next phase
10. About [Your Company] — two-sentence boilerplate

Target total length: 600-900 words. Write in clear, factual prose. Avoid adjectives that cannot be verified. Every claim must be traceable to the inputs provided.
```

## Example Output Description

A structured document of 700-800 words. The headline leads with a number and a timeframe. The challenge section reads like a mini-story with a clear before state. The results section features a stat block with 3-5 metrics in a visual layout, followed by narrative. The quote is the longest single element on the page and is positioned after the results. The about section is two sentences.
