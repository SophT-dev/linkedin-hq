# ICP Definition

<!-- This module defines your Ideal Customer Profile. Paste it into the ICP section of any template. A precise ICP is the single biggest lever for outbound performance - everything downstream (signals, copy, targeting) depends on this being right. -->

## Company Criteria

- **Industry verticals:** [e.g., B2B SaaS, FinTech, HealthTech]
- **Company size:** [e.g., 50–500 employees]
- **Revenue range:** [e.g., $5M–$50M ARR]
- **Geography:** [e.g., US, UK, DACH]
- **Growth stage:** [e.g., Series A–C, scaling post-PMF]
- **Hiring signals:** [e.g., hiring 3+ SDRs, posting for VP Sales, building RevOps team]
- **Tech stack signals:** [e.g., uses HubSpot, recently adopted Snowflake, runs on AWS]
- **Funding recency:** [e.g., raised in last 12 months]

## Persona Targets

| Persona | Title patterns | Seniority | Priority |
|---------|---------------|-----------|----------|
| Primary | [e.g., VP Sales, Head of Revenue, CRO] | Director+ | P0 |
| Secondary | [e.g., RevOps Manager, Sales Ops Lead] | Manager+ | P1 |
| Influencer | [e.g., SDR Manager, AE Team Lead] | IC–Manager | P2 |

## Negative Indicators (Disqualifiers)

- [e.g., Company is pre-revenue / pre-product]
- [e.g., Already using competitor product X]
- [e.g., Fewer than 5 employees]
- [e.g., Government / public sector]
- [e.g., No English-language web presence]

## Scoring Rubric

**Tier 1 (Perfect fit - prioritize immediately):**
- Matches 4+ company criteria
- Primary persona identified
- At least one active buying signal
- No negative indicators

**Tier 2 (Good fit - include in campaigns):**
- Matches 3+ company criteria
- Secondary persona identified
- May have buying signals
- No critical negative indicators

**Tier 3 (Marginal fit - deprioritize or exclude):**
- Matches fewer than 3 company criteria
- Only influencer-level contacts found
- No buying signals detected
- One or more soft negative indicators

## Filled-In Examples

### Example 1: Dev Tools SaaS

**Company Criteria:**
- Industry: B2B SaaS, DevTools, Developer Infrastructure
- Size: 100–1,000 employees
- Revenue: $10M–$100M ARR
- Geography: US, Canada, UK
- Growth stage: Series B–D
- Hiring: Posting for engineering managers, platform engineers
- Tech stack: Uses GitHub, deploys on AWS/GCP, runs CI/CD pipelines

**Primary persona:** VP Engineering, Head of Platform, CTO
**Secondary persona:** Engineering Manager, DevOps Lead
**Disqualifiers:** Consulting firms, agencies, pre-seed startups, companies building consumer products

### Example 2: HR Tech Mid-Market

**Company Criteria:**
- Industry: Professional services, staffing, mid-market enterprises
- Size: 200–2,000 employees
- Revenue: $20M–$200M
- Geography: US, UK, Australia
- Growth stage: Established, 5+ years in operation
- Hiring: Posting for HRIS admin, People Ops, Talent Acquisition leads
- Tech stack: Uses Workday, BambooHR, or legacy HRIS

**Primary persona:** CHRO, VP People, Head of HR
**Secondary persona:** People Ops Manager, HRIS Administrator
**Disqualifiers:** Companies with fewer than 100 employees, early-stage startups, fully remote companies under 50 people
