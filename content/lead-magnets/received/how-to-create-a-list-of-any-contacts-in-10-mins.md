## Key takeaways
- 4-step Clay-based list build: (1) build initial company list, (2) enrich for specific qualifying criteria, (3) combine criteria into one filtered view, (4) export/continue enriching.
- Named example qualifiers you can stack: LinkedIn follower count, monthly web traffic, specific tech usage (e.g. Salesforce), open job postings for sales roles, hosting webinars/digital events (detected via an AI 'Mission' enrichment prompt).
- Concrete Clay AI-prompt example given: 'Tell me if this company hosts webinars, digital summits, virtual events, or online events. Return yes/no.'
- Final qualification = an AND of all checkbox columns (FollowerQualification AND WebTrafficCheck AND UsesSalesforceCheck AND JobOpeningsCheck AND WebinarsCheck), then filter table to only show fully-qualified rows.
- Sells Clay's edge as: multiple data providers/waterfalls, zero-code AI formulas, scalability to thousands of rows, and real-time dynamic filtering.

## Excerpt
Create Your Initial Company List, then Enrich for Specific Criteria: LinkedIn Followers, Monthly Web Traffic, Technology Usage (Salesforce example), Open Job Positions (Sales Roles), Hosting Webinars (AI Web Scraping) — add 'Client' as an enrichment, and in the Mission prompt detail exactly what you want returned (yes/no + link). Combine All Criteria: add one final checkbox that validates all conditions at once, then filter the table to only show qualified rows. Next Steps: export or continue enriching (add Work Email Waterfall for contact emails), then push to an email tool like SmartLead or a LinkedIn outreach platform.
