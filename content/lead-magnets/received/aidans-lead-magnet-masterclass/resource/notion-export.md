### 4.3 Name It Like a Product, Not a File
[verbatim]
Gamma lead magnets got overplayed because they became too easy to make. Everyone started churning out "Free Guide to X" presentations and the market stopped caring. We switched to Notion and immediately saw better response rates. But the format shift was only half the reason.
The other half was the name.
A lead magnet name follows one formula: Desired Outcome + Topic + Container Word. Three to five words. That is it.
Container words that make it feel like something you would buy: Blueprint, Playbook, Kit, Swipe File, System, Vault, Cheat Sheet, Toolkit, Framework, Scorecard.
Examples at different energy levels:
| Weak Name (file name) | Strong Name (product name) |
|---|---|
| Free LinkedIn Guide | The LinkedIn Pipeline Playbook |
| My DM Templates | The 5-Message DM Framework |
| Lead Gen Tips PDF | The Inbound Authority System |
| Outreach Template | The Cold Outreach Swipe Kit |
Notice: every strong name sounds like something you would pay $50 for. Nobody pays $50 for "Free LinkedIn Guide."
YOUR TURN: Rename your lead magnet. Fill in the blanks:
Desired Outcome: [What result does the reader get?] Example: "Pipeline-Building", "Authority", "Inbound"
Topic: [What specific area?] Example: "LinkedIn DM", "Cold Email", "Content"
Container Word: [From the list above] Example: "Playbook", "System", "Kit"
Your Lead Magnet Name: The [OUTCOME] [TOPIC] [CONTAINER] — Example: The Pipeline-Building LinkedIn Playbook
Write down three options. Pick the one that sounds most like something a friend would say "yo send me that" about.
The name is the first thing people see in your giveaway post. If it sounds generic, they scroll past before they even read what is inside.

### 4.4 Write a Hook That Forces the Click
[verbatim]
On LinkedIn, the "see more" fold hides everything after about 140 characters on mobile. Your hook has two lines to earn the click. Two lines. That is not a lot of room to be boring.
From analyzing every lead magnet giveaway post that broke 100+ comments in our space, three hook patterns dominate.
Pattern 1: Credential + Outcome — Lead with the biggest number you can honestly claim, then state what the lead magnet delivers.
Pattern 2: Contrarian Opener — Say something that makes people stop scrolling because they disagree or are shocked.
Pattern 3: Story + Stakes — Start with a specific scenario that the reader has lived through. Pain point agitation.
YOUR TURN: Write three hook variants (each under 140 characters for the first line): Credential + Outcome hook / Contrarian hook / Story + Stakes hook.
Read them out loud. The one that makes you feel slightly uncomfortable to post is probably the right one.
The last sentence of every hook ALWAYS ends with a colon. That colon tells the reader "the good stuff is below the fold." Without it, there is no reason to click "see more."

### 4.5 Stack Your Deliverables So Nobody Can Say No
[verbatim]
After the hook, after the brief context paragraph, comes the deliverable stack. This is where you list out everything inside the lead magnet. And this is where a vast majority of people completely blow it.
They write a paragraph describing what the lead magnet is about. Nobody reads that paragraph. People skim. They are looking for a list that tells them "is this worth my time to comment?"
The winning pattern: Checkmark items. 4-6 of them. Each one line. Include numbers in at least 2 items and parenthetical benefits in at least 2 more. That is the entire formula.
FILLED EXAMPLE (DM framework lead magnet):
- The complete 5-message DM sequence with filled examples
- 12 personalized opener templates (the ones booking 35%+ reply rates)
- The objection-handling scripts for "not interested" and "send me info"
- Our internal follow-up cadence that turns ghosts into booked calls
- The ICP targeting checklist we run before every campaign
FILLED EXAMPLE (content strategy lead magnet):
- The exact weekly posting schedule we run for 30+ clients
- 8 hook formulas that consistently break 50K impressions
- The LinkedIn algorithm playbook (updated for the latest shift)
- Our internal content-to-call tracking spreadsheet
- The DM-to-call bridge script that turns engagement into pipeline
YOUR TURN rules: start each with a checkmark; one line per item, no exceptions; put a number in at least 2 items; add a parenthetical benefit in at least 2 items; if an item sounds vague ("tips for better outreach"), make it concrete ("7 outreach templates with filled examples").
Read your stack out loud. For each item, ask: "Would I comment on a post to get THIS specific thing?" If no for any item, replace it or make it more specific.
Numbers and parenthetical benefits are not decoration. They are what make someone stop, read the list, and think "okay yeah I need that." Vague deliverables get vague engagement.

### 4.6 Build the CTA That Turns Comments Into Calls
[verbatim]
The CTA on a giveaway post has three steps and nothing more. Do not overthink this. Do not make people jump through six hoops.
Winning CTA structure: Like this post / Comment "[KEYWORD]" / Connect with me (so I can DM you). That is it. Three steps.
The keyword should be: ONE word, ALL CAPS, related to the lead magnet topic, easy to type and remember.
| Lead Magnet Topic | Good Keyword | Bad Keyword |
|---|---|---|
| DM frameworks | CALLS | "send me the framework" |
| LinkedIn hooks | HOOKS | "I want the hooks please" |
| Content strategy | CONTENT | "interested in this" |
| Email automation | AUTOMATE | "can I have it" |
| Lead gen system | PIPELINE | "this looks great" |
The single-word keyword does two things: makes commenting frictionless, and creates a wall of identical comments that acts as social proof. "When someone scrolls through and sees 30 people all commenting 'CALLS,' they comment too. That is herd behavior working in your favor."
After they comment: the DM sequence. The actual calls get booked in the DMs that follow. The lead magnet is the bridge into a conversation.
DM TEMPLATE (send within 2 hours of the comment): "Hey [NAME], just sent over the [LEAD MAGNET NAME]. Quick q: what are you working on right now that made you grab this?"
That question opens a conversation, qualifies them, and gives you the perfect segue into a call. Do NOT just send the link and disappear — "that is the equivalent of getting a warm lead and throwing it in the trash."
YOUR TURN checklist: Keyword chosen (one word, all caps, topic-related) / CTA written (3 steps, nothing more) / DM template saved in notes or CRM / Auto-DM set up if available (or manual within 2 hours).
"The giveaway post gets the comments. The DM conversation books the call. If you stop at the comments, you are collecting vanity metrics, not pipeline."
