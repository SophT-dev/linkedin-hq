# Sell Based on Value, Not Volume

**Source:** unknown; explicitly cites Alex Hormozi's '$100M Offers' — https://www.notion.so/Sell-Based-on-Value-Not-Volume-21d0435e95c7804f8988f28028a3caf3?pvs=21
**Topic:** offer-creation · **Type:** notion-page

## Key takeaways
- Uses Hormozi's Value Equation directly (credited): Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice). Increase the first two, decrease the last two.
- Dream Outcome: sell the transformation, not the task — bad example: 'I'll write 3 tweets and 2 threads per week'; better: 'I'll get you 10,000 followers and 2 daily sales calls in 90 days.'
- Perceived Likelihood: give value up front, show proof, use testimonials, address objections before asked — 'people don't just buy information, they buy certainty.'
- Time Delay: give a fast win in week one even if full transformation takes months — momentum builds trust.
- Effort & Sacrifice: 'done-for-you always beats do-it-yourself' — list every step the client would have to do, then eliminate/automate/do it for them.

## Excerpt
The goal isn't to make the most sales. The goal is to make the most money. If you're trying to win by offering the lowest price, you'll blend in, get price-shopped, and end up broke. Use the Value Equation (from $100M Offers by Alex Hormozi): Dream Outcome (↑), Perceived Likelihood of Achievement (↑), Time Delay (↓), Effort & Sacrifice (↓). If you want to raise your prices, don't just charge more. Increase perceived value. Reduce resistance. Make the outcome feel inevitable. That's how you escape price wars and build offers people feel lucky to buy.
