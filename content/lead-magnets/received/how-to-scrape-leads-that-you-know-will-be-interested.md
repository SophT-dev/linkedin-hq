## Key takeaways
- Introduces two named sourcing categories: 'Warm Outbound' (people who already engaged — social followers, newsletter subs, website visitors, post likers/commenters, LinkedIn profile viewers) and 'Near Bound' (leads reachable via shared partner/tech-stack ecosystem, e.g. via a tool like Reveal that syncs partner CRM data).
- Named tool stack: Common Room (social listening on who's engaging with your brand), RBD Toby (IP-based website-visitor identification), PhantomBuster (scrapes post likers/commenters/profile viewers into CSV).
- Full workflow given: PhantomBuster scrape → Clay (Enrich Person + Enrich Company + Work Email Waterfall) → AI-personalized copy → SmartLead (email) or Heyreach (LinkedIn) for send.
- Explicit personalization example lines given: subject 'Saw you liked our recent post—wanted to follow up'; intro 'Hi [FirstName], noticed you've been exploring [Partner Tool].'

## Excerpt
Warm outbound focuses on reaching out to people who have already interacted with you — followed you, joined your newsletter, visited your site, engaged with a post, or viewed your LinkedIn profile. Near bound involves reaching companies in your broader ecosystem — sharing a partner integration or tech stack — via tools like Reveal that sync partner CRM data. Practical example: scrape LinkedIn post likers with PhantomBuster, import into Clay, apply Enrich Person + Work Email Waterfall, customize copy with Clay's OpenAI integration, then push to SmartLead. Why it matters: higher response rates, simple personalization, and efficient use of time versus blasting cold, uninterested prospects.
