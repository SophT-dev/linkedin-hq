# 4 Signs You've Found a Great Market

**Source:** unknown (sub-page inside 'The Ultimate Outbound Sales Playbook') — https://www.notion.so/4-Signs-You-ve-Found-a-Great-Market-21d0435e95c780f48d18e3ea5c7d37e1?pvs=21
**Topic:** offer-creation · **Type:** notion-page

## Key takeaways
- 4-part market-fit test before building any offer: (1) Real pain — the market 'can't ignore' the problem, not just 'wants' a fix; (2) They can pay — explicitly calls out college kids/broke founders/hobbyists as bad targets; (3) They're easy to find — you must be able to name where they hang out, what they read, who they follow; (4) The market is growing — momentum compounds, avoid dying markets.
- Core line: 'You don't need a perfect product. You need people who are desperate for a solution.'
- Bigger pain = faster buying decisions AND higher price tolerance — pain size directly drives both speed and price.

## Excerpt
You could have the perfect offer, the best copy, the smartest funnel… But if the market doesn't need what you're selling, none of it will matter. You don't need a perfect product. You need people who are desperate for a solution. That's the real advantage. So before you start building, ask: Is this a starving crowd? 1. There's Real Pain — you want a market that 'can't ignore' their problem: constant back pain, a sales team missing quota, a founder bleeding cash. The bigger the pain, the faster they buy — and the more you can charge. 2. They Can Pay — college kids won't pay $2K for coaching, broke founders won't hire your agency. 3. They're Easy to Find — where do they hang out, what newsletters do they read, which influencers do they follow, can you run ads/DM/cold email them? 4. The Market's Growing — not a dealbreaker but a big plus; dying markets are a slow bleed.
