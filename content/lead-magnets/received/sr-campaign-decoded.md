# SR Campaign Decoded

**Source note:** SalesRobot. Richest single source in the whole batch — named case studies, explicit benchmarks, reusable 2x2 targeting framework.


## Key takeaways
- SalesRobot's own benchmarks: average manual-outreach client gets 15-30% connection acceptance; SalesRobot's platform average is 50% acceptance and 46% response rate. Most quality campaigns fall 35-50%; even top campaigns rarely exceed 60-65%.
- Recommends 20-30 connection requests/day/account (up to 30/day safely with Sales Navigator); flags <30% acceptance as a targeting/messaging red flag.
- Named case studies: Anton Titov (Pakistani fintech agencies) — 65% response rate, 148 positive replies from 400 prospects; Joey Kennedy (health tech + Vietnam engineers) — 57% overall reply rate, 250+ connections sent; Mattia Bizzoni — 67% connection acceptance, 55% response rate, 25-30 booked calls/month; Alex Sardinha (e-learning) — 54.5% acceptance, 40% response rate, ~100 quality replies via a free complimentary-month offer.
- Message rules: keep under 50 words; connection requests often blank outperform messaged ones; space follow-ups 3-5 days apart; voice notes > video > text in engagement; avoid external links early (spam trigger).
- 2x2 targeting framework (Demand Capture vs Demand Generation x High/Low Awareness): commoditized/high-awareness demand-capture services need differentiation-focused messaging; low-awareness demand-generation offers need heavy education/proof.
